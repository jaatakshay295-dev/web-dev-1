Dinosaurs were a diverse group of reptiles that dominated Earth's land for over 165 million years during the Mesozoic Era. They became extinct approximately 66 million years ago, though their legacy continues in modern birds. The name "dinosaur" was coined in 1842 by Sir Richard Owen and means "terrible lizard," reflecting their size and grandeur.

The lion (Panthera leo) is a large, powerful wild cat native to Africa and India, known for its social behavior and the male's distinctive mane. The lion is the second-largest big cat species after the tiger.
